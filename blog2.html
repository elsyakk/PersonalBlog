    • in the age
    of space

    Astronomy has always been a deep hobby of mine to go down the rabbithole
    to. I wanted to take Aerospace Engineering on 11th grade because of my
    adoration of the Space Rockets, as I never missed most of SpaceX or NASA
    livestream.

    There’s really something that is very peculiar about the space. Staring at the
    dark night out there make me wanders and fills me with curiosity to know
    what’s out there and what are the secrets that the universe hold for us to
    uncover, and the rest that we will never discover.

    James Webb Space Telescope

    I fall in love with Perseverance and Curiosity Rover. As they are on the quest
    on a lonely planet, vastly far away from our home, the earth. It drives me in
    awe into how lonely we are out there while we knew that the possibilities of
    us being alone in the universe and not being alone both are scary.

    I used to want to become an Astro-Physicsts who is dedicating her life for
    science and having the life to uncover the quest of the Universe. There’s so
    much things to discover and I always wanted to know more about the world
    that we live in and anything bigger than us.

    To see all of the accomplishments that the human-kind has made throughout
    the scientific journey drives me into feeling a sort of goosebumps as I know
    that there are much more to uncover. That scary, unfamiliar part of the
    Universe and to the smallest scale of it, smaller than atoms, which is the
    absurdity of quantum.

    I love reading of all of the theories about the Universes such as the Parallel
    Worlds, the String Theories, the Big Bang Theories and the mysteries that
    surround them as I still could not grasp that there was, or was not anything at
    all, that we call “nothing” before all of it.

    It is really intriguing to come up and realise on how insignificant all of us
    are, not even as small as a sand, but we are near nothing. We are just
    universe that is aware of ourselves, as we call it consciousness, while each of
    us trying to find our own meaning of the world.

    All of the things that are significant to us to a great amount is nothing, really
    nothing, compared to everything the Universe held. It’s a reminder that our
    part of being conscious as human being is truly a gift and wonders for us to
    delve more and explore; as we are still an insignificant being that lost in an
    infinite (or not) space.
