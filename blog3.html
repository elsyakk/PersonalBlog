    • but we don't have
    forever

    One of the favorite lies that someone told me is that if I wanted to do/experience
    something, i can find a way to do that in the future. but the problem is,
    I don't live forever.

    I am running out of time. in a place full of questions and despair, everyone
    seems to live day-by-day, not finding meaning through it all. we all distract
    ourselves from the dissatisfaction of life into the short dopamine culture that
    brings us happiness, temporarily, before getting back into the rat race.

    it is easy to become distracted at such things, there are far too many
    entertainments and external influences that stopped us from thinking individually.
    I keep asking myself over and over again, "what do I want out of this life?".

    is it this hustle towards more of everything only to never become satisfied, or am I
    too scared to try out the different path? remind me again, what am I scared
    of? is it the path of being disliked by the mass, which yields to unhappiness
    and a prolonged unfulfilling life.

    we all tend to highlight the community culture amongst us, or the usual "us
    humans are social creature" mindset, but have you ever tried to do the opposite of
    that? from what i've observed, people are too fixated on the "social" part to
    the point that choosing path differently--being an outcast--is seen as a
    strange, unfamiliar thing that must be condemned. is that what we want in
    society?

    to live is to experience many things, positive or negative, and the life that i
    want is on the opposite side of fear. when i found a new thing to get scared
    of, do it scared, facing fears is good for the souls. we have nothing to get scared
    anyways, and nothing to lose either.

    so, when you are faced with options or opportunities that scare you, it's a
    sign of a good change, it's a sign of moving a step forward in life. it's the
    beauty of life, we all have nothing to lose anyways. all of our materialistic wants or
    perception of ourselves are all fake, it's an illusion

    we deserve to be happy and to seek a better life, so go out there, find it,
    and face your fears. mental fears are fake, anyways.
